01 Research understanding

The target is fluency by vocabulary use, not transcription alone.

The study needs a workflow that protects three kinds of evidence: acoustic pause boundaries, verbatim spoken language, and word-level timing that can connect pauses to clauses and multi-word units.

Fluency

Pause behavior

Sounding, silence, pause count, and pause duration come from acoustic boundaries. These boundaries must be reviewed in Praat because they are direct fluency variables.

Vocabulary

MWU evidence

The transcript must preserve spoken wording, including fillers, repetitions, repairs, and false starts. MWU analysis depends on the corrected transcript rather than cleaned-up ASR prose.

Connection

Pause location

The research question asks whether pauses occur within clauses, at clause boundaries, or around MWUs. That cannot be answered from utterance-level transcript alone.

Current interpretation

The revised design treats ASR as a draft generator and Praat as the review environment. The MVP should first prove that the six-tier review file and the reviewed T1/T4/T5 export are reliable.

Word-level alignment, clause-location labels, and MWU-pause relations are valid research extensions, but they require method definitions and a gold subset before they should be presented as deliverables.

02 Updated workflow

The MVP is a six-tier Praat review protocol, with an explicit extension path.

The six-tier TextGrid is not six final answers. It is a structured review interface: two acoustic drafts, two review tiers, and two language drafts. The final research source is the corrected TextGrid saved after Praat review.

Acoustic review block

Tiers 1-3

Fluency draft
T1
praat_sounding_silence Praat CLI acoustic draft. It is the primary review reference, not automatic truth.
T2
local_vad_sounding_silence Local VAD draft. It gives a second acoustic pass and helps expose disagreement.
T3
sounding_silence_review_status Flag tier for T1/T2 conflicts and attention points. It is a review guide, not data.

Tiers 4-6

Vocabulary draft
T4
speaker AssemblyAI diarization draft. The researcher corrects speaker attribution in Praat.
T5
transcript AssemblyAI ASR draft. It should preserve fillers, repairs, repetitions, and false starts.
T6
review_status Flag tier for low confidence, short or unmatched speech, and other review conditions.
Step 01 Draft

Generate the six-tier review TextGrid.

The system runs Praat CLI, local VAD, AssemblyAI diarization, and AssemblyAI ASR. It combines the results into one TextGrid so the researcher can compare acoustic and language drafts in the same Praat view.

Artifact
  • Six-tier draft TextGrid.
  • Method log with acoustic and ASR parameters.
  • Flags for regions that need attention.
Step 02 Review

Correct the core tiers in Praat.

The researcher edits the core tiers directly: Tier 1 for sounding/silence, Tier 4 for speaker, and Tier 5 for transcript. Tiers 3 and 6 point to places that deserve attention, but they do not replace human inspection.

Reviewed source
  • Tier 1: final sounding/silence.
  • Tier 4: final speaker labels.
  • Tier 5: corrected transcript.
Step 03 Save

Save the reviewed TextGrid as the audit source.

After review, the corrected TextGrid becomes the file of record. This is the point where automatic drafts stop being claims and become documented, researcher-checked data.

Research source
  • Reviewed TextGrid.
  • Original audio.
  • Method log and draft generation parameters.
Step 04 Export

Export the MVP spreadsheet from reviewed T1, T4, and T5.

The base Excel does not read the draft tiers. It reads the reviewed sounding/silence, speaker, and transcript tiers only, so the spreadsheet reflects what was checked in Praat.

MVP output
  • Timeline sheet.
  • Summary sheet.
  • No unreviewed ASR rows.
Step 05 Extend

Add word alignment and MWU analysis only after gold inputs arrive.

If the research team wants pause-location or MWU-pause analysis, the next module can run forced alignment from the reviewed transcript and produce candidate pause-context rows. This is outside the base MVP until gold labels and method definitions are supplied.

Extension gate
  • Word-boundary gold sample.
  • Clause-boundary rule.
  • MWU definition or list.
03 Research export

The MVP export should stay simple; advanced sheets are optional.

To stay aligned with the current MVP, the default spreadsheet should not imply that clause-location or MWU-pause analysis is already solved. The base export reads only the reviewed tiers that came out of Praat.

Scope decision

The latest proposal correctly points out that pause location and MWU relations may matter for the final study. However, those outputs require more than the reviewed six-tier file: they need word alignment, clause rules, MWU rules, and validation against gold labels.

Therefore the MVP export should remain conservative. Advanced sheets can be implemented and tested, but they should be marked as candidates until the research team approves the definitions and validation plan.

04 Required input

What we need from the research team to move the MVP forward.

The next milestone is not a bigger feature list. It is a small, representative gold package that lets us calibrate the six-tier drafts and prove how much review time the MVP can save.

Pilot data

Representative audio

Provide a small batch of real recordings with expected speaker count, language context, recording type, and whether overlap should be expected or excluded.

Gold review

Reviewed TextGrid subset

Provide researcher-reviewed T1/T4/T5 for a few files or file segments. This becomes the benchmark for pause boundaries, speaker labels, and transcript quality.

Method settings

Shared conventions

Confirm the silent-pause threshold, transcript conventions for fillers and repairs, speaker-label format, and what counts as an acceptable draft error.

Claims boundary

We can claim now that the MVP creates a six-tier draft for Praat review and exports reviewed T1/T4/T5 into a conservative spreadsheet. We should not claim final pause-location labels, final MWU-pause relations, or MFA word timing as ground truth before gold validation.

The immediate request is therefore practical: send pilot audio, a reviewed gold TextGrid subset, pause and transcript conventions, and acceptance criteria. Once those are available, the MVP can be tuned and measured rather than argued in the abstract.

Suggested reply wording

We agree that word timing, pause location, and MWU relations are important for the full research agenda. To keep the MVP defensible, we propose two layers: first, deliver the six-tier Praat review workflow and a conservative Excel export from reviewed T1/T4/T5; second, activate word alignment and pause/MWU candidate exports only after the research team provides the required gold inputs.

The immediate request is a pilot audio batch, a researcher-reviewed TextGrid subset, the silent-pause threshold, transcript conventions, and validation criteria. Clause-boundary rules, MWU definitions, and a word-boundary gold sample can then unlock the advanced research export without overstating what the MVP has already proven.
